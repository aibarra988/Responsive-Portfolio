About Me

My name is Abraham Ibarra. I am from El Paso, TX and I've been living in Austin since 2012. Some of my favorite hobbies are tinkering with technology, skateboarding, and cooking. I love going to shows in town and traveling to national parks across the US.

As far as I can remember, I've been fascinated by computers, video games and problem solving. I knew my calling was to be a developer. After serving in the US Navy for four years, I've gained five years in the IT industry; three of which I have been a software developer at CA Technologies working on the IDaaS platform CA Identity Service.

During my time at CA Technologies, I was left hungry to keep growing as a software engineer. My goal is to always keep working towards writing clean and useful code while keeping in mind the hardships and lessons that come from working on a dense codebase with brittle tests. One big lesson I learned: always write tests.

I enjoyed being able to contribute to working in all of the components of our tech stack and participate in designing and developing new features. I made the time to sharpen my skills and grow my network by going to The Coding Boot Camp at the University of Texas so that I could feel more confident when interviewing for my next position.
